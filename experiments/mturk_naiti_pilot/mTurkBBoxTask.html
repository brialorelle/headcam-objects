Guide for Labeling

  1. Search for the name of the five most obvious OBJECTS you see (do NOT label people or body parts)
  2. Select the most appropriate label and draw a rectangle around each object, leaving a 2-3 pixel margin.
Click on the “Instructions” button below to see tips and examples of good and bad annotations.

  1. Draw a rectangle using your mouse over each instance of the object.
  2. Do not label people or body parts.
  3. Do not include parts of the object that cannot be seen, even though you think you can interpolate the whole shape of the object.
  4. Avoid shadows, they’re not considered as a part of the object.
  5. If the object goes off the frame, label up to the edge of the image.
